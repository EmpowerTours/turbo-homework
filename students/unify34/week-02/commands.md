## Your Roadmap

Here's how I'd suggest approaching this assignment:

### Step 1 — Get Your Terminal Open
- **Windows**: Launch WSL (search "WSL" or "Ubuntu" in Start Menu)
- **Mac**: Terminal or iTerm2
- **Linux**: Your native terminal

### Step 2 — Explore These Command Categories
Try commands in each area and *take notes as you go*:

| Category | Commands to Try |
|----------|----------------|
| Navigation | `pwd`, `ls`, `cd` |
| File Operations | `touch`, `mkdir`, `cp`, `mv`, `rm` |
| Viewing Files | `cat`, `less`, `head`, `tail` |
| System Info | `whoami`, `echo`, `man` |

### Step 3 — Document What You Did
Your `commands.md` should capture:
- The command you ran
- The output you got
- What it does in plain English

---

## 💡 A Tip on Documentation

Think of your `commands.md` like a **lab notebook**. Show your work! For example, instead of just listing commands, document the *story* of your session.
